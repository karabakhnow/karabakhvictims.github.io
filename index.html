Why you should care about  Victims of Karabakh?

In 1988, Armenia laid a territorial claim to Nagorno-Karabakh, then an autonomous province within Azerbaijan. The flaring up conflict soon engulfed both countries eventually leading to a full-scale war in the early 1990s. As a result, 20% of Azerbaijan’s lands were occupied by Armenia, which was condemned by the UN Security Council in 4 of its Resolutions. Armenia rejected to comply with them and its armed forces carried out ethnic cleansing displacing over half a million of Azerbaijanis and committed a series of grave war crimes. Most of these atrocities against the civilian population of Azerbaijan and occupation of its lands, however, went either unnoticed or underreported by the international community. 

The lack of firm condemnation condoned Armenia to continue its aggression against Azerbaijan and keep under occupation its lands. With the recent reignition of the war since September 27, emboldened by the silence of the international community, Armenia has been targeting Azerbaijan’s civilian population. Several towns and settlements lying outside the conflict zone have been shelled by Armenia via long-range missiles, which have put an end to lives of over 80 civilians. The international outcry is necessary to force Armenia to stop targeting the peaceful population and bring it to justice for the war crimes and avoid repetition of crimes in the future.
